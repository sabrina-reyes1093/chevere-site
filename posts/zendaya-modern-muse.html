Zendaya: The Modern Muse and Turning Fashion Into Story

Jul 5, 2026

Zendaya has never treated the red carpet as simply a place to wear a beautiful dress. With each new project, she and longtime stylist Law Roach create a fashion era that reflects the world of the film. For The Odyssey, her wardrobe has embraced flowing white fabrics, sculptural silhouettes, metallic details and Grecian influences that subtly connect to her role as Athena. Rather than recreating a costume, the looks capture the power, wisdom and mythology associated with the character.

This approach is often called method dressing, but Zendaya and Roach take it beyond matching an outfit to a movie's theme. During the Challengers press tour, tennis-inspired pieces brought the film's athletic world onto the carpet. For Dune, futuristic silhouettes and metallic textures reflected the story's otherworldly setting. Now, The Odyssey has introduced a softer but equally commanding visual language, combining goddess-like draping with details that resemble armor, sculpture and ancient art.

What makes Zendaya's style so effective is her ability to fully commit to an idea without disappearing beneath the clothes. She moves easily between dramatic couture, archival fashion, masculine tailoring and understated gowns. Even her boldest looks feel carefully balanced, with quieter hair, makeup or accessories allowing the clothing to remain the focus. Each appearance feels intentional, building toward a larger story rather than existing as an isolated fashion moment.

Zendaya has ultimately changed what audiences expect from a celebrity press tour. Her outfits introduce a character, establish a mood and generate excitement around a film before viewers enter the theater. Even someone unfamiliar with The Odyssey can understand its sense of mythology, power and grandeur through the imagery of her wardrobe. Zendaya does not simply arrive at a premiere wearing a dress — she gives the audience another scene.
